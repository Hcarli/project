Comparing Economic Growth of Countries Using the Solow Model

The Solow model can be used to compare the economic growth of different countries by analyzing their key economic indicators such as savings rates, population growth, and technological progress. Hereâ€™s a step-by-step approach:

Step 1: Gather Data

Collect data on GDP, capital stock, labor force, savings rate, depreciation rate, population growth rate, and technological progress for each country.

Step 2: Calculate Key Variables

Compute output per worker (\( y \)) and capital per worker (\( k \)) for each country. Determine the steady-state levels of these variables using the formulas:

\[ k^* = \left( \frac{sA}{\delta + n} \right)^{\frac{1}{1-\alpha}} \]

\[ y^* = A (k^*)^\alpha \]

Step 3: Analyze Steady-State Predictions

Compare the steady-state predictions of output per worker and capital per worker across countries. This comparison helps in understanding the potential long-term economic positions of the countries.

Step 4: Evaluate Convergence

Examine whether poorer countries are growing faster than richer ones, as predicted by the conditional convergence hypothesis.

Step 5: Perform Empirical Analysis

Conduct regression analysis with GDP growth rates and other key indicators to empirically test the predictions of the Solow model.

This approach helps in understanding the underlying factors driving economic growth and provides a framework for comparing the growth trajectories of different countries.
